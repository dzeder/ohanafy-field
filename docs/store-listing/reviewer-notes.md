## 3. Feature walkthrough (~3 minutes)

To exercise the full app:

1. **Voice command (mic permission):** From an account's detail screen, tap the **mic button** in the bottom-right corner. Permission prompt fires. Speak: *"Add four cases of Yuengling Lager."* The transcript appears in real time. Tap stop. The AI parses the command and shows a confirmation card. Tap **Accept**. The order draft appears.

2. **Order entry + offline queue (no network needed):** Open the order draft → adjust quantities with +/- → tap **Place Order**. Toggle airplane mode on the device. Place another order — it shows a "Pending Sync" badge. Toggle airplane mode off. The sync queue drains automatically; the badge disappears within 5 seconds.

3. **Bluetooth printer (optional, no hardware needed):** Tap **Print Shelf Talker** on any product. Without a paired printer, the app falls back to **Labelary preview mode** — you'll see the rendered label as a PNG. No Bluetooth pairing or hardware is required to test the label-generation pipeline.

4. **Camera (barcode scan):** Tap **Scan Barcode** on the product picker. Camera permission fires. (No actual barcodes are needed for review — confirm the camera prompt and view fire.)

5. **Biometric lock (Face ID / fingerprint):** Background the app for 60+ seconds, foreground it. Face ID / fingerprint prompt fires. (Optional — can be disabled in Settings.)

## 4. Why each permission is required

| Permission | Why | Required? |
|---|---|---|
| Microphone | On-device speech recognition for hands-free order entry while the rep is at a bar. Activates only on explicit mic-button tap. Audio buffer is processed locally and never leaves the device. | Optional (core flows work without voice) |
| Camera | Scan product barcodes during visits. | Optional |
| Bluetooth | Pair Zebra ZQ520/ZQ630 thermal printers to print shelf talkers and delivery receipts. | Optional (Labelary preview fallback works without printer) |
| Location (When-In-Use) | Surface nearby accounts and route stops. | Optional |
| Face ID / Biometric | App-lock to keep sales data private. | Optional |

**No permissions fire on app launch.** Each prompts only when its specific feature is first used.

## 6. Differentiation (Apple 4.3 spam pre-emption)

Ohanafy Field is **not a generic CRM client**. The differentiating capabilities, none of which exist in Salesforce Mobile App or comparable apps, are:

1. **Offline-first architecture.** Every read works in airplane mode; writes queue. Salesforce Mobile App requires a connection for most operations.
2. **Voice-driven order entry.** Speech-to-AI-to-line-items pipeline. No competitor offers this.
3. **AI-driven account intelligence.** Pre-call insights generated by Claude using account history. Generic CRM apps don't do this.
4. **On-the-spot ZPL label printing.** Direct Bluetooth pairing to Zebra printers. No generic CRM does this.

The app is designed for a specific user (beverage distributor field rep) doing a specific job (visiting 8–12 accounts/day with a Zebra printer on their belt). The value proposition is operational, not informational.

## 7. Bluetooth justification (Apple 5.1.5 — explicit)

> The Bluetooth permission is used solely to pair with Zebra ZQ520 and ZQ630 Bluetooth thermal printers, manufactured by Zebra Technologies, for printing physical labels (shelf talkers, product feature cards, delivery receipts) during in-person sales visits. The app does not use Bluetooth Low Energy beacons, does not track user location via Bluetooth, does not connect to any other device categories, and does not transmit any user data over Bluetooth other than the ZPL label content being sent to the user's own paired printer.

## 8. Microphone justification (Apple 5.1.1)

> The microphone is used solely for on-device speech recognition (`@react-native-voice/voice` library) to enable hands-free order entry while the rep is at a bar or warehouse. Audio is processed locally on the device using Apple's Speech framework (iOS) or Android's SpeechRecognizer (Android). The raw audio buffer is never persisted, never transmitted to any server, and never shared with third parties. Only the resulting text transcript is stored, in the user's local app database. The mic activates only when the user explicitly taps the mic button.
